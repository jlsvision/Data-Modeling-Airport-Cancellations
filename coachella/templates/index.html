AIRPORT CANCELLATIONS

U.S. Airline Cancellation Analysis 2015 ~ 2018

Have you ever gotten your wonderful trip plan ruined due to an airline cancellation? For example, how many people suffered from the snow storm that hit major airports in 2018? Which caused cancellation of more than 550 flights.

There are many reasons why flights get cancelled; bad weather as we mentioned, security, lack of passengers, lack of aircraft, crew out of time, lack of crew or pilot, mechanical issues and more. All of these many reasons are unpredictable and the airline has no choice but to cancel the flight and disrupt trips.

Assumption and prediction

Our assumption is that every month and year there are certain days that airline gets cancelled more often. During the winter months from December to March.

Mission

Our mission is to use the given data to visualize the data and find a trend or pattern of cancellation of the air flights over the years and months. We hope to use the pattern to help airlines and travel companies to account our data to their risk management team and make the best decisions for customers.

Data Sets

Toolkits: This project heavily relies on pandas and Numpy for data wrangling, analysis, and visualization. Our database is based from SQL. The primary plotting library we will explore is Plotly;

1. International IATA Airports Data: Airport Data from Rapid API. The dataset consists of reliable airports list and details, with IATA codes. The Data is accessed through an API call.

2. Airline Data: Airline Data from the Kaggle public data website. The dataset contains a database of over 5000 airlines. The data presents airline name, IATA id, callsign, and country.

3.U.S Airline Delay and Cancellation Data from 2009 to 2018: Airline Delay Data from the Kaggle public data. The dataset is derived from the Department of Transportaions flight info of US domestic flights. Updated yearly, showing data for the entire years of 2009 to 2018.
